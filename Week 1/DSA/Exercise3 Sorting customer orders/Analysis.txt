 Analysis

1. Bubble Sort compares adjacent orders and swaps them if needed.

2. Bubble Sort has a best-case time complexity of O(n).

3. Bubble Sort has an average and worst-case time complexity of O(n²).

4. Quick Sort divides the orders into smaller parts using a pivot.

5. Quick Sort has an average time complexity of O(n log n).

6. Quick Sort can have a worst-case time complexity of O(n²).

7. Quick Sort performs faster than Bubble Sort for large datasets.

8. Quick Sort requires fewer comparisons and is more efficient.

9. Therefore, Quick Sort is generally preferred for sorting customer orders.
